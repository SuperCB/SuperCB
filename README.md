>The  checker shadow illusion  is an  optical illusion  published in 1995 by  Edward Adelson , an American professor of  vision science  at the  Massachusetts Institute of Technology . The phenomenon features an image of a  checkerboard  with light and dark squares, partly shadowed by another object, such as a cylinder as in this illustration. The optical illusion is that the area labeled A appears to be darker than the area labeled B.
