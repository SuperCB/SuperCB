>Wulfenite  is a lead  molybdate  mineral with the  chemical formula  PbMoO 4 . It is most often found as thin tabular crystals with a bright orange-red to yellow-orange color, sometimes brown, although the color can be highly variable. In its yellow form it is sometimes called "yellow lead ore". The mineral was first described in 1845 for an occurrence in  Bad Bleiberg , Austria, and was named after  Franz Xaver von Wulfen , an Austrian mineralogist.
