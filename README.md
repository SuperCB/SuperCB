>The  sebae anemone  ( Heteractis crispa ) is a species of  sea anemone  belonging to the family  Stichodactylidae . It is widespread throughout the tropical and subtropical waters of the  Indo-Pacific , from the eastern coasts of Africa and the  Red Sea  to  Polynesia , and from south Japan to Australia and  New Caledonia . It prefers hard-base  substrates  slightly covered with sand but it can also cling to branching  corals  from the surface to a depth of 40 metres (130Â ft). This sebae anemone was photographed in  Ras Muhammad National Park , Egypt. A tiny  Red Sea clownfish  can be seen among the anemone's  tentacles  in the centre of the image.
