It is  metastable  at  standard temperature and pressure , converting to the  chemically stable  form  graphite  under those conditions but at a negligible rate. Diamond has the highest  hardness  and  thermal conductivity  of any natural material, properties that are useful in major industrial applications such as cutting and polishing tools. In addition to its industrial uses,  diamond is much sought-after as a gemstone  for use in jewelry.
