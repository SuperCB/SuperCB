>Phoenicolacerta troodica , commonly known as the Troodos lizard or the Troodos wall lizard, is a species of  lizard  in the family  Lacertidae . It is endemic to Cyprus, where it is common and widespread and its natural habitats are  Mediterranean-type  shrubland and rocky areas, in both rural and urban areas. Its length is up to 22 centimetres (8.7 inches), with males slightly larger than females. The species has a long tail, which can grow to more than twice its body length.
