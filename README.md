>Sea urchins  are a group of  spiny  globular  echinoderms  which form the class Echinoidea. About 950 species live on the seabed, inhabiting all oceans and depth zones from the  intertidal  to 5,000 metres (16,000 feet; 2,700 fathoms). Their  tests  (hard shells) are round and spiny, typically from 3 to 10 centimetres (1 to 4 inches) across. Sea urchins move slowly, crawling with their  tube feet , and sometimes pushing themselves with their spines. They feed primarily on  algae  but also eat slow-moving or sessile animals. Their  predators  include  sea otters ,  starfish ,  wolf eels , and  triggerfish .
